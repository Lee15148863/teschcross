How to Find Your Model Number

Knowing your exact model helps us give you an accurate repair quote

🍎 Apple (iPhone / iPad)

4 ways to find your Apple device model number

Method 1: Settings App

  1. Open Settings
  2. Tap General
  3. Tap About
  4. Look for Model Name (e.g. iPhone 14 Pro) and Model Number
  5. Tap the Model Number once to reveal the full "A" number (e.g. A2650)

Method 2: Physical Device

  1. Look at the back of your device
  2. Find the small text near the bottom — it shows the model number (e.g. A1901)
  3. For newer iPhones (8 and later), the model is printed on the SIM tray instead

Method 3: Original Box

  1. Check the label on the original packaging
  2. The model number is printed next to the barcode

Method 4: Device is Off or Broken

  1. Remove the SIM tray — the model number may be engraved on it
  2. Or check your Apple ID account at appleid.apple.com → Devices
💡 Tip: The "A" number (e.g. A2650) is the most accurate way to identify your device. We use this number for pricing.

🤖 Android (Samsung, Huawei, Xiaomi, OPPO, Google, OnePlus, etc.)

4 ways to find your Android device model number

Method 1: Settings App

  1. Open Settings
  2. Scroll down and tap About Phone (or About Device)
  3. Look for Model or Model Number (e.g. SM-S918B, CPH2451)
💡 On Samsung devices, go to Settings → About Phone → Software Information for more details.

Method 2: Dial a Code

  1. Open the Phone (dialer) app
  2. Type *#06# — this shows your IMEI number
  3. Search the IMEI at imei.info to find your exact model

Method 3: Physical Device

  1. Check the back of the phone or under the battery (if removable)
  2. The model number is usually printed in small text
  3. Also check the SIM tray — some brands print it there

Method 4: Original Box

  1. Find the label on the original packaging
  2. The model number and name are printed next to the barcode
💡 Tip: If your phone won't turn on, the original box or SIM tray is the easiest way to find the model.
