1. Purpose

This notice sets out the relationship between StoreFlow and the customer regarding the customer's MongoDB Atlas database. It must be read, understood, and accepted before StoreFlow will connect to or deploy services using the customer's MongoDB Atlas connection string ("URI").

2. MongoDB Atlas Setup Summary

  1. Create a free MongoDB Atlas account at mongodb.com/atlas.
  2. Create a new cluster (free M0 tier is sufficient for small shops).
  3. Create a database user with read/write permissions. Store the username and password securely.
  4. Under Network Access, add your IP address or allow access from anywhere (0.0.0.0/0).
  5. Click "Connect" → "Drivers" → copy your connection string. It looks like: mongodb+srv://user:pass@cluster.mongodb.net/dbname
  6. Replace user, pass, and dbname with your actual values.
  7. Provide this URI during StoreFlow registration.

3. Free Cluster Limitation Warning

MongoDB Atlas free tier (M0) clusters are limited. They provide up to 512 MB of storage with restricted performance and features. They may not be suitable for high-volume production use, advanced backup schedules, point-in-time recovery, or certain security compliance requirements. Customers are responsible for monitoring their Atlas usage and upgrading to a paid tier before reaching storage or performance limits. StoreFlow is not responsible for service degradation caused by Atlas free tier limits.

4. Customer Responsibilities

The customer acknowledges and agrees that they are solely responsible for:

  • Account ownership and billing: The MongoDB Atlas account is owned by and billed to the customer. StoreFlow does not pay for or subsidise Atlas costs.
  • Database users and passwords: Creating, rotating, and securing database credentials.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA): Enabling MFA on the Atlas account.
  • Network access rules: Configuring IP whitelists and network access controls.
  • Data backups and exports: Performing regular backups and exports of POS data, including sales records, invoices, cash ledgers, inventory, repair records, reports, and business data.
  • Region selection: Choosing the appropriate Atlas deployment region for data residency requirements.
  • Compliance: Ensuring the use of Atlas complies with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.
  • All data stored in the database: The customer owns and controls all data. StoreFlow does not have access credentials beyond those provided by the customer.

  • StoreFlow may mask or redact the URI in logs, user interfaces, and API responses.
  • StoreFlow does not access customer data except as necessary to provide, maintain, and support the service, or as directed by the customer.
